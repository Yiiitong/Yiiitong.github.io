Submission 18:
This paper aims to demonstrate the efficacy of a multi-scale, progressive learning strategy for statistical shape modeling, based on their previous work DeepSSM. 
The authors propose several architectures to implement their method, in which the Unet-Backbone with segmentation-guided multitasking progressive leaning proves the most effective in performance enhancing. They bring up the interesting idea of the fusion of bottleneck and decoder features in Unet backbone for a more robust representation.
However, the authors primarily compare their new method with their own previous work (DeepSSM) and a standard segmentation architecture. Including more comparisons with other SOTA methods would be recommended to provide a more comprehensive view of how the new method performs.


Submission 21:
The authors present new developments in their new version of open-source shape analysis software package, SlicerSALT v4.0, as compared with previous versions. New features including skeletal representations, registration-based correspondence, shape population viewer, shape variation analyzer, distance weighted discrimination, deep learning for geometry and new infrastructure SlicerPipelines. 
The paper provides a clear overview of the SlicerSALT software package and its capabilities. The software package provides a wide variety of tools for creating different shape representations and performing statistical analysis. It would be helpful if the authors could provide a more detailed comparison with existing tools in the field, highlighting the unique features and capabilities of SlicerSALT.

The paper gives an interesting and plausible method for automatic dental scan registration. It provides a clear and detailed description of the methodology, including the orientation workflow, the creation and training of the Butterfly patch in the upper dental arch, the final automatic registration. However, there exists a large standard deviation in their error results, indicating that the results may not be stable. The authors mention this in the discussion section but do not provide insights into the reason for this variability or possible directions for improvement.
