**2. Brain Imaging Generation with Latent Diffusion Models** (https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.07162) (MICCAI Workshop on Deep Generative Models)
no codes yet ==*** *==
- training with ***UK Biobank*** MRI data, to learn about the probabilistic distribution of brain images, conditioned on covariables, such as age, sex, and brain structure volumes
+ 160 × 224 × 160 voxels as input
+ using *LDM*: combine the use of autoencoders to compress the input data into a lower-dimensional latent representation with the generative modelling properties of diffusion models.
	+ trained the *autoencoder* with a combination of L1 loss, perceptual loss, a patch-based adversarial objective, and a KL regularization of the latent space
	+ The encoder maps the brain image to a latent representation with a size of *20 × 28 × 20* with 3 latent channels. -> After training the compression model, the latent representations of the training set are used as input to the diffusion model (denoising diffusion model, LDM with the **DDIM** sampler) 
		1. compression model had a similar architecture to Stable diffusion, but  substituted the 2D convolution with **3D** convolutions, and removed all attention layers from the compression model
		2. diffusion model uses the **U-net** architecture, used a Markov chain with 1000 timesteps, with a linear variance schedule, from 0.0015 to 0.0205. 
		3. We used a hybrid approach for **conditioning** with a **spatial transformer** with a depth of 1 and 1 **attention** heads.
	+ conditioned our models according to age, gender, ventricular volume, and brain volume relative to the intracranial volume.
		To perform this conditioning, we used a hybrid approach combining the *concatenation* of the conditioning with the input data and the use of *cross-attention mechanisms*

**3. Generating Realistic 3D Brain MRIs Using a Conditional Diffusion Probabilistic Model** *(Wei Peng, Ehsan Adeli, Qingyu Zhao, and Kilian M. Pohl)* ==*** *==
no codes yet
+ a conditional DPM with attention to generate an MRI sub-volume (a set of slices at arbitrary locations) conditioned on another subset of slices from the same MRI
+ By using a mask to encode the arbitrary locations of the condition and target slices, the model is able to learn *long-range dependency* across distant slices such that the generated novel samples have strong consistency in brain anatomy and intra-sequence variability of real MRIs.
+ ==After training, the DPM generates a new MRI by generating the first few slices from random noise and then generating subsequent slices conditioned on the existing slices. ==
+ DPM: The optimal neural network is determined by minimizing the reconstruction loss of the noise $\epsilon$ for any given sample $X_t$ at any time $t$

our network $\epsilon_\theta$

+ randomly sample two sets of slices from $X$ to construct the condition group, $X^C$, and the target group, $X^P$ with corresponding slice index sets $C$ and $P$.
+ no constraint of the number of slices in $C$ and $P$. Instead, we introduce $Tmax$ as the maximum of the total number of slices in these two groups, and make $len(C) + len(P) \le Tmax$. There must be at least one slice in $X^P$ to be our generation target during training, but $X^C$ can have an arbitrary number of slices as long as it satisfies the aforementioned constraint.

+ After training our generative model, a new independent 3D MRI is generated by progressively applying the trained DPM starting from random noise:
	+ First, the beginning slices of a MRI volume are produced from random noise. 
	+ Then, based on the generated slices, DPM is executed for $N - 1$ times to synthesize successive MRI slices, where $N$ is a hyperparameter. At each stage, expected slices are produced conditionally on slices generated from the previous stage.

**Network $\epsilon_\theta$** is constructed by an U-net architecture with multi-head self-attention mechanism:
+ $\epsilon_\theta$ is built by basic 2D resNet blocks, attention modules, and upsampling/downsampling modules. The skip connection is added for better convergence. Time step (noise level $t$) input is encoded as an embedding by a time embedding module: $Embedding(t) = [cos(tw^{\frac{2d}{Q}}); sin(tw^{-\frac{2d}{Q}})]$
  -> with $Q$ being the number of dimensions of the embedding and $d = 1; 2; ...; Q/2$ being the set of values. $w$ is normally set to $10000$ to control the minimum frequency of the embedding.
+ With a residual block built by an **fully connected layer**, the $Embedding(t)$ is fed into every U-net layer with the corresponding dimensionality. 
+ For the **attention** module, instead of learning a position embedding that considers slices separately, our attention weights are computed from slice indices to increase resolution in the third dimension.

**Dataset**: *1262* brain MRI from *ADNI*, UCSF, SRI international 
	Sub-volumes are created by splitting the MRI along the *axial* direction.
